The Argentina MacriMeter

EMPEZAR

English version

El presidente de Argentina, Mauricio Macri tomó las riendas del poder el 10 de diciembre de 2015, después de doce años de las políticas populistas de los presidentes Néstor Kirchner y Cristina Fernández de Kirchner. Estos años han dejado al país con muchos retos complejos, sobre todo en políticas económicas. Las reformas que Macri inició en sus primeros días en el cargo, están dirigidas a traer a la Argentina de vuelta a la racionalidad económica y de su reinserción en la economía mundial.

La siguiente sección es una lista de los pasos y las reformas más importantes que se están llevando a cabo en la Argentina, las cuales están siendo planificadas y ejecutadas por el gobierno de Macri. Dicha lista nunca puede ser definitiva. Esta lista pretende ayudar al público a entender las opciones que están sobre la mesa, tomando en cuenta que todas las reformas progresaran a un ritmo independiente. Esta lista será actualizada mensualmente mientras vemos progresos tangibles en cada reforma individual.

Eliminate all export duties

In Progress

DIFFICULTY: Low

Eliminating all export duties makes Argentine agricultural commodities more competitive in international markets.

In 12/2015, The Macri government eliminated export taxes on all commodities excluding soybeans and soy products. Under the decree, export taxes on soybeans and soy products were reduced by five percent.

Removal of state subsidies for household consumption of electricity and gas

Complete

DIFFICULTY: Medium

Implement a new tariff policy that reduces subsidies by $4 billion dollars, decreasing supply costs and encouraging private investment.

President Macri imposed the new policy that eliminated subsidies for utility rates stating that the previous subsidies increased supply costs and discouraged private investment

Increase the minimum nontaxable income threshold

In progress

DIFFICULTY: Medium

Increase minimum non-taxable income, appeasing unions amid inflation and increasing the income tax brackets.

02/2016 the government increased the minimum nontaxable income by 160% from $462 (ARS 6,938) to $1,257 (ARS 18,880) for single persons.

Access to sovereign credit markets

Complete

DIFFICULTY: High

Reach agreement with bondholders who had not accepted 2005 and 2010 swaps for restructured bonds.

On 02/29/2016, the government agreed to pay $4.65 billion dollars to its four largest creditors, led by Elliot Management.

Issue bonds in order to begin debt servicing

In Progress

DIFFICULTY: High

Permit the government to issue bonds and allow for the return of international markets.

02/2016 U.S Judge Thomas Griesa agreed to drop injunctions that prevented Argentina from issuing new bonds or servicing its restructured debt. Congress agreed in 03/2016 to remove the Lock Law and Sovereign Payment Act. 04/2016 Argentina issued $16.5 billion in international bonds.
